You can't celebrate Cinco de Mayo without our famous Mexican street-style corn dip that is absolutely to die for. Sweet corn and cotija cheese team up to create the tastiest appetizer dip ever. Don't forget the to-go plates because this dip is definitely left-over-worthy!

  • Ingredients:
    • 3 cups corn kernels, (1 pound) fresh or frozen
    • 1 4 ounce can roasted chiles
    • 3 tablespoons mayonnaise
    • ½ cup crumbled cotija cheese
    • 3 tablespoons sour cream
    • 1 teaspoon chile powder
    • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
    • ½ teaspoon kosher salt
    • 2 tablespoons cilantro, chopped
    • 1 cup shredded Mexican blend cheese

  • Recipe:
    1. Grill the corn in its husks for 4-5 minutes or until tender and fragrant. Cut the corn from the cob and discard the cobs. (or, heat the frozen corn in the microwave for 3 minutes or until warm.)
    2. Add the corn to a large mixing bowl. In a small bowl, mix the chiles, mayonnaise, sour cream, chile powder, garlic powder, and kosher salt.
    3. Fold in 1 ½ tablespoons of the cilantro and half of the cotija cheese. Fold the mayonnaise mixture into the corn.
    4. Fold in 1 cup of shredded Mexican blend cheese. Spoon into an oven-proof serving dish.
    5. Preheat the oven to 375°F and bake for 20 minutes or until the dip is bubbly and the cheese is melted.
    6. Garnish with the remaining cotija cheese and cilantro. Sprinkle with a few dashes of chile powder.
    7. Serve with tortilla chips and enjoy!
